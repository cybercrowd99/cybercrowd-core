Status

Discovery In Progress

This document preserves current observations regarding Identity within CyberCrowd.

Identity is not considered complete.

This document is expected to evolve as additional observations, replay events, life lanes, and participation models are discovered.

---

Purpose

Identity is the continuity anchor of a human being within CyberCrowd.

Identity is not a profile.

Identity is not a resume.

Identity is not a social media account.

Identity is not a reputation score.

Identity is not a collection of tags.

Identity is the persistent container that allows a human to move across Life Lanes while remaining themselves.

---

Current Observations

Identity currently appears to involve:

- Continuity
- Replay
- Presence
- Authority
- Life Lanes

Additional organs may exist and remain undiscovered.

Current observations should not be interpreted as a complete Identity model.

---

Life Lanes

Identity may participate in one or more Life Lanes.

Examples include:

- Friends
- Stories
- Travel
- Food
- Work
- CyberJobs
- CyberServices
- Creator
- Education
- Collectibles

Life Lanes are places an Identity goes.

Life Lanes are not the Identity itself.

A lane may change.

The Identity remains.

---

Presence

Humans are often remembered by presence rather than records.

Examples:

- The person who never stopped smiling.
- The storyteller.
- The builder.
- The teacher.
- The helper.
- The friend everybody remembers.

Presence is currently considered a first-class observation and may become a dedicated Identity organ in the future.

---

Replay

Replay preserves moments.

Replay is not limited to achievements.

Replay may include:

- Stories
- Memories
- Lessons
- Mistakes
- Victories
- Running jokes
- Relationships
- Important events

Replay exists to preserve continuity rather than perfection.

---

Authority

Identity belongs to the individual.

Authority determines who may:

- View
- Participate
- Request access
- Receive access

Authority remains under the control of the Identity owner unless explicitly delegated.

---

Play And Work

CyberCrowd currently recognizes a distinction between:

- Play
- Work

These are separate contexts.

A human may be playful in one lane and professional in another.

The separation exists to preserve context rather than create restrictions.

Security may require separation.

Separation should not become unnecessary bottlenecks.

---

Alexandria ON

A working concept under exploration.

Traditional archives preserve information.

Alexandria ON seeks to preserve:

- Presence
- Participation
- Continuity
- Replay

The objective is not merely to store records.

The objective is to prevent people from disappearing when a lane, platform, service, or era ends.

---

ON

Current working observation:

ON is genuine participation.

ON is not merely being logged in.

ON is not merely being active.

ON is the state of being present, engaged, and part of the show.

Examples include:

- Building
- Teaching
- Learning
- Playing
- Helping
- Creating
- Sharing

---

Design Rule

Identity should become stronger through use.

Identity should not become more difficult through use.

Security may introduce separation.

Separation must not become unnecessary friction.

The user experience should move toward participation rather than bottlenecks.

---

Discovery Rule

Archive does not mean complete.

Observation does not mean final.

Identity remains under active discovery.

Future observations may expand, modify, reorganize, or replace portions of this document while preserving the reasoning trail that led to those observations.
